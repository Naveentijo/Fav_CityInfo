Iconic Attractions

Golden Gate Bridge

An iconic 1.7-mile suspension bridge connecting San Francisco to Marin County. Perfect for walking, biking, or simply admiring the stunning views.

Alcatraz Island

Historic former federal prison located on a rocky island. Take a ferry tour to explore the cells and learn about famous escape attempts.

Chinatown

The oldest Chinatown in North America and largest outside of Asia. Explore authentic restaurants, shops, and cultural landmarks.

Fisherman's Wharf

Bustling waterfront area with fresh seafood, street performers, souvenir shops, and the famous sea lions at Pier 39.

Golden Gate Park

1,000+ acre urban park featuring gardens, museums, lakes, and recreational areas. Home to the de Young Museum and Japanese Tea Garden.

Coit Tower

Art Deco tower on Telegraph Hill offering 360-degree views of the city and bay. Features beautiful Depression-era murals inside.

Things to Do

Union Square Shopping

Premier shopping destination with luxury boutiques, department stores, and world-class dining options in the heart of downtown.

Lombard Street

Experience the "most crooked street in the world" with eight hairpin turns and beautiful landscaped gardens.

Ferry Building Marketplace

Historic ferry terminal turned gourmet marketplace featuring local artisans, farmers market, and specialty food vendors.

Mission District Murals

Explore vibrant street art and murals that tell the stories of San Francisco's diverse Latino community and culture.

Japanese Tea Garden

Peaceful 5-acre garden in Golden Gate Park featuring traditional landscaping, pagodas, and authentic tea ceremonies.

Cable Car Rides

Ride the historic cable cars, the only moving National Historic Landmark, through the city's steep hills and scenic routes.

Annual Events & Festivals

Chinese New Year Parade
February

One of the largest celebrations outside of Asia, featuring elaborate floats, lion dancers, and a spectacular 268-foot Golden Dragon.

San Francisco Pride
June

Colorful celebration of LGBTQ+ community with parades, festivals, and events throughout the city, culminating in the famous Pride Parade.

Outside Lands Music Festival
August

Three-day music and arts festival in Golden Gate Park featuring top artists, local food, wine, beer, and art installations.

Fleet Week
October

Military celebration featuring the Blue Angels air show, ship tours, and various military demonstrations around the Bay Area.

Hardly Strictly Bluegrass
October

Free three-day music festival in Golden Gate Park featuring bluegrass, folk, country, and roots music on multiple stages.

Fog City Cycle
September

Annual cycling event taking riders through San Francisco's most scenic routes, including the Golden Gate Bridge and waterfront areas.
